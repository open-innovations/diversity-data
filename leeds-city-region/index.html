Socio-economic background

This section uses categories from the Social Mobility Commission toolkit which suggests employers ask the key question "What was the occupation of your main household earner when you were about aged 14?". The options include: lower socio-economic background (e.g. technical and craft occupations; routine, semi-routine manual and service occupations; long-term unemployed); intermediate background (e.g. clerical and intermediate occupations; small business owners); professional background (e.g. modern professional & traditional occupations; senior or junior managers or administrators).

Reference area values come from 2021 Census data for National Statistics Socio-economic Classification which includes usual residents aged 16 years and over. Previously we used data split by AB/C1/C2/D/E approximated social grade classifications. In the 2021 Census data we have grouped National Statistics Socio-economic Classification categories as follows:

  • Lower - L10 and L11 Lower supervisory and technical occupations, L12 Semi-routine occupations L13 Routine occupations, L14.1 and L14.2 Never worked and long-term unemployed, L15 Full-time students
  • Intermediate - L7 Intermediate occupations, L8 and L9 Small employers and own account workers
  • Professional - L1, L2 and L3 Higher managerial, administrative and professional occupations, L4, L5 and L6 Lower managerial, administrative and professional occupations

Note that these classifications are for individuals as of the 2021 Census rather than the classification of their parents when they were 14 as this is the best proxy we have available for this question.

About the dashboard

This Leeds City Region Diversity Dashboard is a work-in-progress project for the Leeds Anchors Network. It uses data published in our agreed diversity data standard (version 1.1; 2021-11-10). The data standard was drawn from existing diversity data published by members of the Leeds Anchor but has attempted to map characteristics to existing national standards as much as possible so that we can make comparisons with nationally curated data sets e.g. the UK Census.

Adding your data to the dashboard

If you publish diversity data about your organisation within the Leeds City Region you can get your data included here by following the publishing guidance. First make sure that you create a CSV file with column headings that match those in our data standard - we have created a Diversity Data Builder tool to help. There should be a row per date/group/level within your organisation (which could be as simple as one row giving figures for your entire organisation). The data should be published openly on the web at a static URL. For ease, we suggest that you publish the file on Data Mill North with the tag #DiversityData. Once your data is published openly, it needs to be added to the dashboard index. If you have a Github account you can create a pull request directly or you may contact us at Open Innovations to get it added manually.
