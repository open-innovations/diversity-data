Religion

This section shows religions as collected by the 2021 Census in England which asks: What is your religion?. Christian includes Church of England, Catholic, Protestant and all other Christian denominations. Other includes any other religions not otherwise listed.

Socio-economic background

This section uses categories from the Social Mobility Commission toolkit which suggests employers ask the key question "What was the occupation of your main household earner when you were about aged 14?". The options include: lower socio-economic background (e.g. technical and craft occupations; routine, semi-routine manual and service occupations; long-term unemployed); intermediate background (e.g. clerical and intermediate occupations; small business owners); professional background (e.g. modern professional & traditional occupations; senior or junior managers or administrators).

Reference area values come from 2011 Census data for approximated social grade. We have grouped C2 and DE as Lower, C1 as Intermediate, and AB as Professional.

Sexuality

This section has options limited to those offered by the Australian Bureau of Statistics / Government Statistical Service guidance on reporting statistics about sexual orientation.

The 2011 Census doesn't provide data on sexuality so the sexuality figures come from the ONS Population Survey (2018). This is only published by UK Region (e.g. Yorkshire and The Humber) so we have used the regional values.

About the dashboard

This Leeds City Region Diversity Dashboard is a work-in-progress project for the Leeds Anchors Network. It uses data published in our agreed diversity data standard (version 1.0.1; 2021-07-06). This data standard was drawn from existing diversity data published by members of the Leeds Anchor but has attempted to map characteristics to existing national standards as much as possible so that we can make comparisons with nationally curated data sets e.g. the UK Census.

Adding your data to the dashboard

If you publish diversity data about your organisation within the Leeds City Region you can get your data included. First make sure that you create a CSV file with column headings that match those in our data standard. There should be a row per date/group/level within your organisation (which could be as simple as one row giving figures for your entire organisation). The data should be published openly on the web at a fixed URL. For ease, we suggest that you publish the file on Data Mill North with the tag #DiversityData. Once your data is published openly, it needs to be added to the dashboard index. If you have a Github account you can create a pull request directly or you may contact us at ODI Leeds to get it added manually.
